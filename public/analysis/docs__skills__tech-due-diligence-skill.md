# Technical Due Diligence Skill

This skill provides a structured framework for conducting formal "Due Diligence" on any new technology, tool, or framework prior to adoption. It balances **Strategic Innovation** with **Operational Stability** to ensure that architectural decisions are both future-proof and reliable for enterprise-grade applications.

## When to Use
- Prior to the adoption of a new framework or major technology stack component.
- Evaluating third-party APIs, external services, or strategic vendor platforms.
- Conducting a technical audit of a codebase or tool as part of a formal strategic review.
- Assessing the risks associated with major architectural transitions or dependency upgrades.

## Workflow

### 1. Strategic Contextualization
- Define the primary business or technical problem the technology intends to solve.
- Establish the scope of "Minimum Viable Adoption" (MVA).
- Validate alignment with existing enterprise standards and technology roadmaps.

### 2. Multi-Dimensional Risk Assessment
- Evaluate the technology across the **Critical Assessment Categories** defined below.
- Utilize a "Risk Indicator" system (Low Risk, Managed Risk, High Risk).

### 3. Executive Synthesis & Recommendation
- Perform a cost-benefit analysis (considering Performance, TCO, and Time-to-Market).
- Deliver a formal recommendation (Approve, Defer, or Reject) with a comprehensive risk mitigation strategy.

---

## 🏗️ Critical Assessment Categories

### 1. Market & Ecosystem Viability
*Focus: Long-term sustainability and industry adoption.*
- **Market Share & Growth**: Analyze adoption trends, community sentiment, and industrial footprint.
- **Maintenance Velocity**: evaluate the frequency of updates, core team responsiveness, and patch cycles.
- **Ecosystem Maturity**: Availability of professional services, community libraries, and verified integrations.
- **Institutional Backing**: Assessments of corporate stewardship or foundation-led governance models.

### 2. Architectural Integrity & Performance
*Focus: Systemic robustness and scalability.*
- **Architectural Standards**: Adherence to proven design principles (e.g., Modular, Event-Driven, Microservices).
- **Interoperability**: Ease of integration with disparate systems and legacy infrastructure.
- **Performance Characteristics**: Throughput, latency under stress, resource efficiency, and concurrency models.
- **Data Governance**: State management protocols, persistence mechanisms, and consistency guarantees.

### 3. Security, Compliance & Licensing
*Focus: Risk exposure and regulatory alignment.*
- **Vulnerability Profile**: Review of CVE history, security disclosure policies, and audit trails.
- **Protective Mechanisms**: Native implementation of industry-standard security protocols (Encryption, AuthN/AuthZ).
- **Intellectual Property & Licensing**: Legal verification of license compatibility (e.g., Apache 2.0, MIT vs. GPL) and proprietary constraints.
- **Supply Chain Security**: Assessment of transitive dependencies and software provenance.

### 4. Operations & Infrastructure Lifecycle
*Focus: Deployment efficiency and total cost of ownership (TCO).*
- **Infrastructure Overhead**: Resource requirements, cloud-native readiness (K8s/Serverless), and networking complexity.
- **Observability Framework**: Built-in support for telemetry (Logging, Tracing, Metrics).
- **Deployment Automation**: Compatibility with CI/CD pipelines and Infrastructure as Code (IaC) standards.
- **Support Lifecycle**: Stability of APIs, versioning strategy, and quality of migration documentation.

### 5. Personnel Capability & Human Capital (UX/DX)
*Focus: Team productivity and talent acquisition.*
- **Documentation Standards**: Comprehensiveness, clarity, and availability of advanced implementation guides.
- **Skill Acquisition Curve**: Time-to-productivity for senior engineering staff.
- **Developer Ecosystem**: Availability of diagnostic tools, IDE support, and testing frameworks.
- **Community & Support Channels**: Enterprise-level support options and professional forums.

---

## 📜 Core Principles
- **Evidence-Based Evaluation**: Decisions must be supported by empirical data and verified performance metrics.
- **Strategic Alignment**: Technology choices must directly support long-term business objectives.
- **Risk Mitigation**: All identified risks must have a corresponding mitigation or fallback strategy.
